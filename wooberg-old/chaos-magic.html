Chaos Magic

Chaos Magic is a set of additional rules that can be used on top of almost any other format, and it's what inspired the Planechase product. The identifying feature of this variation is its plethora of forced random effects at every turn.

BASIC RULES
  • At the beginning of the game, roll 1d20, and look up that number on the EnchantWorldLand (EWL) list. The game begins with an EnchantWorldLand object in the command zone, with the text of that roll. Effects of an EWL can function from the command zone. When the roll is replaced by a new EWL (or explicitly ended), the object ceases to exist.
  • There is a new phase before the beginning phase of each turn, called the chaos phase. Players receive priority during the chaos phase, but can't cast spells or activate abilities at that time except when an effect instructs it (or, in the case of mana abilities, if it asks for a payment of mana).
  • At the beginning of the chaos phase, the active player rolls 1d200 (or, since he probably doesn't have a die with that many sides, rolls other dice such as a d20 and d10, so that the results can be mapped into the range of 1-200 with equal probability). Look up the result on the chaos list, and put the appropriate chaos roll on the stack, choosing modes and targets as necessary. Putting a chaos roll on the stack is mandatory (as long as there are sufficient targets; if there aren't then nothing happens and you don't roll again). It's possible to respond to chaos rolls, but usually this only happens in the case of the special actions of sacrificing RollFork and CounterRoll counters.
  • Anyway, when the roll resolves, the active player carries out its instructions, which may involve additional rolls on the chaos list, the EWL list, or a third list called PersonaLand, which is a 1d20 list of generally positive effects. Such rolls will likewise go on the stack, and in the case of an EWL roll, its resolution moves it to the command zone.


EFFECTS
  • You can make your own lists of effects for when your group plays Chaos Magic. Even the sizes of the lists can be changed.

Alternate Chaos Lists by Abe Sargent:  http://www.starcitygames.com/magic/misc/23610_The_Kitchen_Table_375_Chaos_Magic.html
