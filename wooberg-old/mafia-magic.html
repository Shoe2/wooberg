Mafia Magic

In this Magic: the Gathering format, players take on the role of either a cunning, secretive member of the mafia or a clueless but determined civilian. The two factions battle to eliminate each other through investigative reasoning, alliances, and timely betrayal. An uninformed majority and an informed minority face off in this extremely political variant of Magic: the Gathering. This format is similar to the party role-playing game mafia, except it has been modified to incorporate Magic: the Gathering gameplay and strategy. It has a somewhat complicated setup phase, but features greatly increased opportunities for intrigue and tricky tactics during gameplay.

Preparations At the beginning of the match, an impartial third-party [hereby known as the gamemaster, or GM] creates a number of index cards equal to the number of players, each reading either 'mafia' or 'citizen'. Players are randomly seated at the table and given one index card, which determines their role for the match. Distribution of mafia and citizen players is somewhat variable and up to the gamemaster's discretion, but the number of mafia players should always be greater than 1 and less than n/2 where n is the total number of players. Generally, 1/4 to 1/5 of total players being assigned the role of mafia will produce a balanced and interesting game.Each player then receives a face-down emblem with the text simply being 'mafia' or 'citizen' according to the role [it's easiest to just use the index cards to represent the emblems] These emblems are visible only to the player who controls them and may not be revealed to any other player during the course of the match. The player who is playing first also receives a face-up 'first player' emblem.

After this, the GM instructs each player to put his or her head down on the table and close his or her eyes. The GM then instructs each player who controls a mafia emblem to raise his or her head and look around. This allows players controlling a mafia emblem to acknowledge each other and observe who they are working with. Citizens are not allowed to look up at this time and must play the game without knowing who is on their team and who is secretly conspiring against them. The GM then instructs the mafia players to put their heads back down (so as to be indistinguishable to the citizen players) and then instructs all players to raise their heads and begin the game.

Gameplay Gameplay then proceeds as a normal game of Magic: the Gathering would, except there are a few additional rules regarding payer roles. In terms of traditional Magic rules, each player may attack any other, there is an unlimited range of influence, and all standard Magic rules for the chosen format apply.

Additional rules-Whenever a player would lose the game, instead he or she turns his or her face-down role emblem face-up, then loses the game. If he or she had a 'first player' emblem, the player to his or her left gains a 'first player' emblem.

Night Phases

A Night Phase occurs under two conditions:-one or more players lose the game-If a player with a 'first player' emblem begins his or her sixth upkeep step since the beginning of the game OR since the last night phase occurred.

All events that occur during a night phase are special actions and cannot be responded to. The night phase allows the mafia team to eliminate a pesky or powerful citizen player.

During a night phase, the GM instructs all players to put his or her head down on the table and close his or her eyes. The GM then instructs each player who controls a mafia emblem to raise his or her head, open their eyes, and point to the citizen player which they most want to be rid of. Each mafia player non-verbally communicates with the other mafia players until a consensus has been reached and all mafia players are pointing to the same citizen player. At this point, the GM instructs all mafia players to put their heads down, then allows all players to raise their heads. The GM then announces which player was selected by the mafia, and that player loses the game. This game loss is a special action and can't be responded to or prevented in any way [yes, this means that a player with a Platinum Angel or a player whose opponent controls an Abyssal Persecutor will still lose]. Play then resumes with the current active player, or if that player lost the game during the night phase who would play next.

Winning When the last player of either faction is eliminated, the game ends. Each player who controlled an emblem of the remaining faction wins the match, and each player who controlled an emblem of the eliminated faction loses. Note that it is possible to lose the game of Magic: the Gathering and still win the match; this is different from most Magic variants.

Strategy There have been countless manuals and even a few dissertations written on mafia strategy. As a mafia member, how do you help your team win without showing your true intentions to the citizens? As a citizen, how do you isolate and identify mafia members so that they are defeated instead of your numerous (but potentially untrustworthy!) citizen allies. As a mafia member, it is important to choose night phase targets wisely so as to eliminate strong citizen threats, and as a citizen it is important to proceed carefully and make sure not to eliminate each other.

On the Magic: the Gathering side, this is a multiplayer format in which combo decks are amazingly weak. The reason for this is that if a citizen goes off and kills a player, they will surely be killed in the resulting night phase; conversely, if a mafia member goes off, they will be able to wipe each citizen in sequence and win the game instantly. Since each player is more likely to be a citizen than a mafia member, this makes combo a less dominant strategy than in most multiplayer formats. The ability of the mafia to kill off citizens also puts an upper cap on the time it takes to play a game, because the number of players in the game gradually decreases over time. This further encourages citizens to be aggressive and play with aggressive decks and strategies that would normally not be strong in multiplayer Magic.

Variations Anyone who is familiar with the game of mafia knows that there are multitudes of variants for any mafia game played. The citizens could be given a doctor, who can choose one player during each night phase to be protected and immune from mafia kills. Or a detective could be added to the game, who could choose one player each night and learn the identity of any one player. For a full list of possible roles, go to en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mafia_(party_game) or search online for a wiki devoted to the game of mafia. Practically any variation on the game of mafia can be applied to Mafia Magic. Just have any special roles take standard night actions during each night phase, and you should be good to go.

If at all possible, it is recommended that rule 600.4a is modified to allow spells and abilities on the stack controlled by a player who is leaving the game to exist until they resolve. This can't prevent the game loss, but it can allow a player who is going to lose a chance to use spells and abilities in a way that would help his or her team achieve a more advantageous position.

Speed variant

For those who feel slightly bogged down by the rules, ignore all night phases and all instances of triggering them. The game becomes a simpler test of a strong citizenry versus a cohesive mafia organization. In this version, the mafia must win based on teamwork and alliance manipulation alone.
